### **Cloud Computing: Efficiency and Sovereignty**

**[Cloud Computing] — Wasm-Cloud Overtakes Containerization for Serverless**
WebAssembly (Wasm) has officially moved from the browser to the backbone of the cloud. Engineering teams are increasingly bypassing Docker containers in favor of Wasm modules for serverless microservices. The primary driver is the "cold start" problem, which has been reduced from seconds to microseconds. With the release of the Component Model 2.0, the interoperability between languages (Rust, Go, Python) within the same Wasm runtime has reached production-grade stability.
**Kink:** The browser’s greatest export is now the cloud’s most efficient and secure runtime.
**Why it matters:**
*   Drastic reduction in infrastructure overhead and cloud spend.
*   Universal portability across hyperscalers without configuration drift.
*   Massive scaling density, allowing thousands of modules per single node.

**Kink:** Green computing has evolved from a PR stunt to a core architectural constraint for global clusters.
**Why it matters:**

**[DevOps] — The Transition from Observability to Self-Healing Clusters**
We are witnessing the first wide-scale production deployments of "Autonomous SRE" agents. These systems have moved beyond simple alerting; they now utilize closed-loop feedback to diagnose, patch, and verify infrastructure failures without human intervention. **Kink:** The role of the DevOps engineer is shifting from "fixing the system" to "auditing the autonomous fixer."
**Why it matters:**
*   Virtually eliminates 3 AM on-call rotations for standard infrastructure failures.
*   Dramatically reduces Mean Time to Recovery (MTTR).
*   Allows human talent to focus on architectural innovation rather than firefighting.
